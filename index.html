Institutions Mission


TALIKALA, INC. NGO catering to prostituted women

The Talikala group was founded with the intention of assisting prostituted women and empowering them in order to break free from their "enslavement".

Their main policy or goal is to guide and empower prostituted women and to alter how the public views them. They achieve this by spreading awareness and educating the public on issues relating to women's and children's rights, reproductive health, gender sensitivity, anti-prostitution, online sexual abuse/exploitation, and improving the leadership skills of community-based groups.

To generalize, the program helps and promotes human rights by empowering people that are victims of prostitution and sex trafficking.



Talikala's Herstory


Women have been primarily targeted for prostitution, sex trafficking, and sexual assault.

Many people have made vulgar remarks or held negative perspectives toward prostitution victims. That affected their mental and physical lifestyle, whether from the trauma or the discomfort they had been through.

Therefore, the Talikala institution was created to help and guide women who have been victims of these in asserting their rights, regaining their dignity, and changing people's perspective towards them in society, thereby making human rights and gender equality be restored.






Project Head


The organizer of the Talikala group is their institution's head, Jeanette L. Ampog.

As the executive director at Talikala incorporated, she carries out tasks such as overseeing the day to day management of the organization, writes and submits project proposals and project reports, generates resources for the organization, prepares and submits reports to the Board of Trustees, and represents the organization to other organizations.

According to their organization, Talikala has a total of 21 founding members who are trained peer educators who focus on teaching about reproductive health, educational assistance, child care, and alternative livelihood.

“Talikala facilitated the formation of a self-help group of women who are victims of sexual exploitation.”








Program Beneficiaries


Most of the people being admitted to the institution are adult women within the age range of 20 to 50 years old, there are also children who are 6 to 12 years old and 13 to 19 years old teenagers.

These people have experienced sexual abuse, exploitation and trafficking, and prostitution which have resulted in problems in their reproductive health.

Their rights, dignity, self-honor, and self-respect have been taken away from them because of the ill and cruel treatment of others. Therefore needing a lot of support to gain themselves back and stand for their human rights.




Partners/Benefactors


Talikala has partnered with many women and youth-related groups.

These include Sangguniang Kabataan, Pag-asa Youth Organizations, GKK, and the Barangay Council of Women.

The institution has also been involved with 9 children organizations, as well as parents and fellow community residents.

Lastly, they are also in close relation with government agencies that also advocate against violence on women and children.
